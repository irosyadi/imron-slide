THE UNIT CIRCLE
TRIGONOMETRY REVIEW



Figure 1.  The Unit Circle

 Remember that sine and cosine were defined using the mnemonic SOHCAHTOA,

Sine is Opposite over Hypotenuse
Cosine is Adjacent over Hypotenuse
Tangent is Opposite over Adjacent


Opposite refers to the length of the vertical or green line, and adjacent refers to the length of the horizontal or red line.

sin(phase) = opposite / hypotenuse

cos(phase) = adjacent / hypotenuse


In the case of the unit circle, the length of the hypotenuse (blue line) is always equal to one.

sin(phase) = opposite / 1

cos(phase) = adjacent / 1
