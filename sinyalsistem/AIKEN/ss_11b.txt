In the context of signal analysis, the terms "power" and "energy" are used as a general convention. What does the text explicitly state about their relation to physical quantities?
A. They always directly represent physical power and energy.
B. They are only used when the signals represent electrical phenomena.
C. They are used as a general convention, often independent of whether they relate to physical energy.
D. They are used only for continuous-time signals, not discrete-time signals.
ANSWER: C
